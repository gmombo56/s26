Why Record Trash?

The reason I decided to record my trash was that I wanted to know what types of items I am throwing out in a week. It can be easy to mindlessly throw away things without thinking about why or what will happen to those items. Especially because many people today do not separate their items into the proper disposal categories.

I was particularly inspired by a few articles I read about trash and the way we think about it. Professor Sarah Newman writes about how our current concept of waste has not always existed. When we think about items found from old societies, we have the tendency to assume they are trash but this is often not the case. These items could have been very significant to the people that used them or they could have been left behind on purpose as an offering.

I also read a NYT article where Vik Muniztalks about how we can reimagine our trash to have different uses. In today's society there has been a rise in the tendency to buy items and only keep them for a short time. Once these items end up in a landfill we often think they can no longer be used but Muniz shows this does not have to be the case. We can reimagine our waste in new and creative ways.

By the end of the week, I am hoping to have a better understanding of the items I decide to throw out. I know that trash can say a lot about a person, such as their hobbies, interests, foods they eat, etc. I would like to be a more mindful consumer and see if there's a way for me to reduce my impact on the environment, whether it’s through repurposing items or buying second-hand items in the future.

I read Emily Batdorf's article where she documented her week of trash and I thought doing a similar reflection myself would be helpful. In her reflection she points out how easy it is for us to contribute to the waste problem without realizing it. When we try to be more mindful consumers we an make simple swaps that in the long run will have a huge impact on the environment.

About The Author

Grace Mombo

Grace Mombo is currently a sophomore at Rutgers University. She is majoring in cognitive science with minors in business and theater. She is interested in learning more about how she can lower her impact on the environment. It has become very normalized to buy products, use them once and then throw them out. As consumers it is important we start to do our part in taking care of the environment. If our habits with waste continue as they are we will get closer to the point of having irreversible;e damage to the environment. Grace hopes that by making this website, she can help people to reflect on their trash and start to make people more mindful consumers.
